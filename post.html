The Perfect Beginner Guitar

saijeanfrench

Mar 22

2 min read

Finding the perfect guitar for your child, or for yourself is super important.

You want to feel comfortable playing the guitar you're holding, it should be challenging getting used to the positions and applying pressure to the strings on the fretboard. With that being said it shouldn't be nearly impossible, don't set yourself up for failure.

Don't invest in a low quality guitar. Brands are important when it comes to instruments Guitar being one of them. A low quality guitar will give you problems with staying in tune, more pain for your hands, durability. Most importantly it won't be comfortable on your hands and that just comes with how the guitar is made which revolves back to quality. So save yourself some time and invest in a good quality guitar.

You don't have to do much research because I will tell you now that Yamaha makes great guitars from electric to acoustic and those guitars can be found on Facebook marketplace for less than $100 sometimes. Brand new you'll be looking at at least $130. If you're buying an acoustic guitar. Electric guitars get a little more pricey just because there's more equipment required. With an electric guitar you need a cable, and amplifier. Versus on an acoustic guitar you don't need any of those things. I want students to feel comfortable with the guitar their holding so I leave it up to the student to decide if they would like to play an acoustic or electric guitar. Personally, I think it comes down to what kind of style of music you'd like to play. Overall acoustic and electric guitar are the same in a theory sense but stylistically they are very different.
